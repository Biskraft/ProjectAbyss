3 Lessons from a Real-time, Turn-based Game.
The game is "The Legend of Bum-bo",
the latest project from Edmund McMillen and James Id.
It's a spin-off of "The Binding of Isaac",
but instead of a twin-stick shooter,
it's a match-four puzzle game... sort of.
It's a very strange hybrid that combines
turn-based strategy with real-time elements.
And through its development and final release,
there are three key lessons we can learn
about game design.
Lesson One: Juicing the Puzzle.
Most puzzle games, like Candy Crush or Bejeweled,
rely heavily on "juice" to make matching gems feel satisfying.
The explosions, the sounds, the cascading animations—
they all contribute to a feeling of impact.
"Bum-bo" takes this even further.
Every time you match symbols,
something physically happens in the game world above.
Matching teeth makes Bum-bo throw a bone.
Matching poop creates a barrier.
This direct link between the puzzle board and the combat
makes every move feel consequential.
It turns an abstract puzzle into a visceral battle.
Lesson Two: The Importance of a Clear UI.
In a game with so many overlapping systems—
matching, spell casting, enemy movement—
a clear UI is vital.
The developers struggled with this during production.
Early builds were cluttered and confusing.
They eventually realized that they needed to
standardize the way information was presented.
Every status effect has a clear icon.
Every enemy's intent is shown before their turn.
By making the rules of the game transparent,
the player can focus on the strategy
rather than trying to figure out what's happening.
Lesson Three: Embracing Randomness.
Being a rogue-lite, Bum-bo is built on randomness.
The items you get, the enemies you face,
the layout of the board—it's all randomized.
But rather than letting randomness ruin the game,
the designers use it to create unique situations.
Every run feels different because the tools you have
force you to adopt different strategies.
If you get a powerful defensive spell early on,
you might play more cautiously.
If you get a high-damage offensive item,
you might go for a glass cannon build.
This "controlled chaos" is what keeps rogue-lites
engaging for dozens of hours.
So, while "The Legend of Bum-bo" is a weird game,
its design choices are rooted in sound principles.
Give your actions impact,
make your information clear,
and use randomness to fuel creativity.
Thanks for watching.

'범보'는 여기서 한 걸음 더 나아갑니다.

퍼즐 판과 전투 사이의 이 직접적인 연결은 모든 움직임을 의미 있게 만듭니다.
추상적인 퍼즐을 생생한 전투로 탈바꿈시킨 것이죠.

퍼즐 맞추기, 주문 시전, 적의 이동 등 수많은 시스템이 얽혀 있는 게임에서 명확한 UI는 필수적입니다.
개발자들은 제작 과정에서 이 문제로 꽤 고생했습니다.
초기 빌드는 복잡하고 혼란스러웠죠.

모든 상태 이상 효과에는 명확한 아이콘을 넣었고, 적의 의도는 턴이 시작되기 전에 미리 보여주었습니다.
