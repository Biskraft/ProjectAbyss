A detective demo inspired by Geoguessr.
Today, I want to talk about a small but brilliant demo
that I recently played.
It’s a detective game, but it’s heavily inspired by 
the mechanics of "Geoguessr".
For those who don’t know, Geoguessr is a game
where you’re dropped into a random Street View location
and have to guess where you are in the world
based on clues in the environment.
This demo takes that core idea of "environmental deduction"
and applies it to a crime scene.
The Concept: Deduction through Observation.
Instead of looking at maps and street signs,
you’re looking at personal belongings, discarded receipts,
and the layout of a victim's apartment.
Every object in the scene has a story to tell.
The game doesn’t provide markers or "detective vision".
You simply have to observe and deduce.
Why it Works.
By removing the "hand-holding" found in many modern detective games,
this demo makes you feel like an actual investigator.
Finding a clue feels like a genuine achievement
rather than just clicking on a prompt.
It rewards patience and attention to detail.
It shows how a simple mechanic—
guessing a location based on visual clues—
can be expanded into a deep and engaging
narrative experience.
It’s a perfect example of how to take inspiration
from one genre and apply it to another
to create something completely fresh.
I can’t wait to see where this project goes.
Thanks for watching.
