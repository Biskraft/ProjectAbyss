# Deathsaber

thumb|300px|Deathsaber's artwork from Makai Kingdom 
The **Deathsaber** is a monster class first introduced in *Makai Kingdom* with the name of Hell Kitty. It first appeared in the *Disgaea* series with *Disgaea 2: Dark Hero Days*.

## Appearance


## Characteristics
It is in fact a demonic version of the Slumber Cat, it has sharper red sabers-teeth, bright red claws, big bat-like ears and demon-like wings and pointy tail. They also lack the characteristic bell.

## Gameplay
Like their animal counterpart, they are an attack-based monster class whose high speed and movement range make up for their usually low HP and defense.

## Appearances in the Disgaea series
### *Disgaea 2: Dark Hero Days*
*Main article: Deathsaber (Disgaea 2)*
While absent from the original *Disgaea 2: Cursed Memories*, Deathsabers appear as a monster class in *Dark Hero Days*. They brandish a fish as a mace for their normal attacks.
Since they have an ability that makes their attacking power to increase by the amount of spaces they have moved, equipping Shoes on them would be a wise choice.
They can Magichange into fists.

### *Disgaea 4: A Promise Unforgotten*
*Main article: Deathsaber (Disgaea 4)*
The Deathsaber appears in this game as a DLC monster class along with Evil Ranger Pink. They magichange into fists. They are also a flying class, able to move through enemies.

## Other Games
### *Makai Kingdom*
Catsabers have low defense and HP, but with high Strength and Move stats. Their mastered weapons are Katanas, Nunchucks, Morningstars, and Fishing Poles. They give bonuses to SP, Move, and Strength when reincarnating.

### *Zettai Hero Project: Unlosing Ranger VS. Darkdeath Evilman*

DeathSaber appears like an enemy, their special ability gives them the chance of decreasing the actual MC's level, a DeathSaber is a default NPC in the Dark Clinic.

## Trivia
- Evil Ranger Pink is a recolor of the Deathsaber Class, and serves as the tutor for the Axel Mode's special functions.
- Hell Kitty is an obvious play-on of *Hello Kitty*.
