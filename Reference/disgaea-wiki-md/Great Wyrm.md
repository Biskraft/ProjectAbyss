# Great Wyrm

thumb|Artwork of the Great Wyrm.
The **Great Wyrm** (also called **Entei** and **Efreet**) is a recurring monster class first introduced in *Disgaea: Hour of Darkness*.

## Appearance
Great Wyrms are depicted as gargantuan bipedal dragons with flames coming out of their backs. 

## Characteristics


## Gameplay
This class is one of the most powerful in terms of physical might, making them perfect for the front line.

## Appearances
### *Disgaea: Hour of Darkness*
*Main article: Great Wyrm (Disgaea)*
The class itself doesn't appear until rather late in the game (Bonus Stages, Item World and Dark Assembly only); however, the Alt Overlord that appears in the map Writhing Shadow has the sprite of a Great Wyrm. Due their infrequent appearances and great strength, this class is one of the hardest Monster Classes to unlock.

### *Disgaea 2: Dark Hero Days*
*Main article: Great Wyrm (Disgaea 2)*
While not present in *Disgaea 2: Cursed Memories*, the class appears once again in the PSP version of *Disgaea 2: Cursed Memories* and *Disgaea 2 PC*. Great Wyrms are unlocked after Axel beats and recruits a Blue Great Wyrm (dubbed the Greatest Actress Ever) in Axel Mode. The class has gained an ability that boosts the Wyrm's power for every other ally Wyrm out. They can also Magichange into a Axe.

### *Disgaea D2 A Brighter Darkness*
*Main article: Entei (Disgaea D2)*
This class returns in *Disgaea D2*, renamed to Entei. They aren't found until the second stage of chapter 10, though. Their evilities are all fire-related, as well as many of their skills.

### *Disgaea 5: Alliance of Vengeance*
*Main article: Dragon King*
The class returns in *Disgaea 5*. Instead of being named as "Great Wyrm", it has the name "Dragon King".

## Trivia
- In *Phantom Brave*, a scene shows Scarlet the Brave killing a Great Wyrm. The class itself however doesn't appear in any map. This is the second appearance of the Wyrm in a Nippon Ichi game and the last appearance until *Disgaea 2: Dark Hero Days*.
- The names of some of the tiers, such as Efreet, Shaitan, and Marid, are types of Jin, well-known creatures in Arabian mythology and lore.
