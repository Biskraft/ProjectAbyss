  - Hoggmeiser** is a character from *Disgaea: Hour of Darkness* and its remakes. His class is related to the Nether Noble monster class.
A former butler to King Krichevskoy, after the King's death, Hoggmeiser gathered up a vast sum of the King's wealth and built his own castle with it, creating a barrier to keep intruders out.

## Profile
### Appearance
Hoggmeiser is a demon with pig-like features. He has red eyes. He has a muscular body, a snout, two folded horns jutting upwards on each side, tiny bat wings on his back and hooves. He wears a red crown and a fur cape fastened by a gold emblem. His design is the basis for the Nether Noble class.

### Personality
Hoggmeiser is a greedy and forgetful demon, but he shows a thread of good will when Laharl spares his life. He is also shown to be extremely proud of his son Porkmeister, who is much braver than he is. (As remarked by both Laharl and Etna in their respective modes.)

## Story
### *Disgaea: Hour of Darkness*

At first, Hoggmeiser intended to become the Overlord, but he was more attached to money than the title and, apparently, his son. After the battle at the end of chapter 3, just as Laharl was about put an end to him, Hoggmeiser's son, Porkmeister, intervened and prevented this from happening. His son was upset at him for being obsessed with money and the title of Overlord.

Laharl, after some persuasion from Flonne, allows Hoggmeiser to live. Touched by Laharl's kindness, he joins the player's party without consent.

Alternately, if 50 allies were killed, Laharl can choose to kill Hoggmeiser, which causes Flonne to intervene. This ending causes Lamington to remark that his plan has ended.

In the remakes' Etna Mode, Hoggmeiser's role is exactly the same as in *Hour of Darkness*. Instead of a kind moment after the battle, however, Etna ties up Hoggmeiser and his son remarking that she will not separate the two, yet they will be her slaves. Therefore, Hoggmeiser will again join without consent. Flonne watches from the sidelines, determined to show Etna the true way of being a Space Detective (Which she thought Etna was at the time.)

## Trivia
- Hoggmeiser's name is a combination of the English words *hog *and *miser*, spelled in a way that may suggest it is German.
- Hoggmeiser's Japanese name, Zeniski (ゼニスキー *Zenisukī*) is a pun on 銭好き (*zeni suki*), meaning "loves money." The name ending in "-ski" may also be meant to suggest that it is Polish. "Zeni" is an old Japanese term for money. This fact is played on in *Disgaea 2: Cursed Memories* and *Disgaea 3: Absence of Justice*, as calling up Hoggmeiser via Cell Phone gets you talking to "Zeniski Financial Services."
- Hoggmeiser and Maderas are only storyline characters from Disgaea: Hour of Darkness that couldn't be unlocked in Disgaea 3: Absence of Justice as secret characters or through DLC, mostly because they both have rather minor roles in the game.
