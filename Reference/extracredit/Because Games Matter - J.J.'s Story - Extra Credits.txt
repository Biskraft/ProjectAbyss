every year around this time we share
stories of people whose lives have been
profoundly impacted by games showing the
real world the good that games can do so
this year we're going to start with JJ's
story my name is JJ Bouchard I'm a
certified Child Life Specialist
certified therapeutic recreation
specialist and the patient technology
coordinator at CS Mott Children's
Hospital in Ann Arbor Michigan when I
was seven I was diagnosed with type 1
diabetes and I spent a week at the very
Hospital I work at now I was told I
could no longer eat candy and that I'd
have to get multiple shots every single
day but video games helped me get
through it and now I use video games to
help children face illness every day
that week in the hospital I got poked
many times I was scared I hated the food
but my second day there an activity
therapist named Jerry Reed invited me to
the hospital playroom and it was then he
introduced me to my first video game
ever pitfall for the Atari 2600 I was in
love I spent as much time in the
playroom as I could and I quickly made
friends with other patients who were
also excited about the game while
playing my friends and I would talk
about our experiences in the hospital
and I no longer felt as scared or alone
then when I went home video games became
a big part of my life my mom even used
the promise of purchasing Double Dragon
2 for the Nintendo as incentive for me
to give myself my first insulin
injection fast forward 20 years and
inspired by Jerry and his amazing
ability to use video games and toys to
make the hospital a fun place I became a
certified Child Life Specialist my job
is to help normalize the hospital
experience for patients and families we
provide patients with games and
activities to help pass the time we
teach them coping techniques for dealing
with painful procedures and we actively
engage them in play well things like
shots and stitches are being performed
we also help them cope with the
realities of death and dying it can be a
very intense job but at the very core of
it we're simply trying to make the
patient's feel safe empowered and find
some joy when I started most Child Life
specialists use traditional toys like
bubbles I spy books and television to
help distract patients during painful
procedures now these techniques worked
well for younger children but tweens and
teens often had a hard time due to the
lack of engaging
looking back at my history I remembered
how much videogames helped me when I was
struggling with injections so I began to
bring Xbox 360s game boys and wheeze
into the procedure rooms for patients to
use while they were getting spinal taps
IV starts and stitches at first the
nurses looked at me like I was crazy but
once they saw how engrossed the patients
were in the games and how compliant they
were during the procedures they all got
on board many patients who used to kick
and scream when they had to get poked
now sat still and even reported not
feeling the injection because they were
so engrossed in the game we soon began
to see the benefits of gaming outside
procedures support patients who I played
video games with were much quicker to
open up and talk about their hopes and
fears it was as if having a video game
to play while talking made the
counseling process less formal and
intimidating patients also became much
more active while playing games because
instead of lying in bed watching TV they
were sitting up and using fine and gross
motor movements to solve problems and
the games had an added social benefit as
well
patients would often gather around a
screen in her activity room and get to
know each other through play and
patients family members had something to
do with them when they came to the
hospital in fact many of the patients
siblings who were initially scared to
visit were now excited to visit the
hospital to come play games with their
brother or sister then in 2013 I was
inspired by a lecture given by Lou Boris
the patient technology coordinator at
Mount Sinai Kravis Children's Hospital
whose primary role was to support all of
the tablets and gaming technology that
the Child Life team was using and he was
able to educate the team about gaming
technology so that they could better
integrate it into their clinical
practice and it made me wonder what if I
a trained clinician could do something
similar at my hospital then in 2015
things really took a turn for the
amazing I was working my dream job
running an activity room at CS Mott
Children's Hospital the very role Jerry
had had 25 years earlier when the Mott
Golf Classic charity announced that they
were going to be putting an Xbox 360 in
every patients room here was my chance
to advocate for a position solely
dedicated to overseeing all of the
recreational technology in the hospital
and educating the staff on how to
integrate that technology into their
therapeutic practice and to my delight
our administrators loved the idea
they created the position for me and
with funding from the motto
classic the CS Mott therapeutic gaming
and digital technology program was
created and over the next three years I
worked tirelessly to engage our local
gaming community to help support our
patients and families and after-school
program named game start school began
providing bedside coding lessons and VR
experiences the nonprofit gamers
outreach started a program called player
2 where local gamers could volunteer to

arcade where we invited all of the
patients and their families to play
social video games on big projector
screens while enjoying snacks and each
other's company we typically get between
50 and 80 patients attending every event
then in 2016
Eric bland in from the child's play
charity visited our Hospital and said he
was inspired by the work people like Lou
and I were doing at the time Boston
Children's Hospital was the only other
hospital that we knew of with a similar
position but then the child's play

positions like ours all over the world
since then they have provided grant

including two more at CS Mott Children's

diagnosed with diabetes admitted to the

thanks to those faithful turn of events
