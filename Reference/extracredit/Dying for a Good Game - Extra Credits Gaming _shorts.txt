for decades we've thought of matchmaking
in exactly one way how do we match you
against an opponent of approximately
your skill in a short amount of time and
most all of our design efforts have been
towards making marginal improvements to
that process but what if we radically
rethought the parameters of how we
approach matchmaking what if we try to
match you for the most enjoyable game
rather than the fastest most
competitively balanced one because think
about it I know for me there are lots of
times when I'm hopping into a casual
game of something and I'd much rather be
on a team with a handful of awesome
people whose play style and reason of
play matched mine even if they were a
bit worse at the game than me rather
than suffer through a half hour of rage
and toxicity with people who are
technically better at no scoping or
something or to be consistently grouped
with people that had similar interests
and social decorum meaning that in-game
chat was actually an option worth
