Games like Balders's Gate 3, Clare
Obscure, and even Hell Divers 2 have
proven that games with a budget in the
tens of millions can compete with games
with hundreds of millions of dollars
behind them. But how is that possible?
Well, first, it's technology. Just being
able to make a game look good used to
cost an enormous amount. But in the last
few years, advances have been less about
making graphics better and more about
making them cheaper. In fact, today
players probably get more fidelity out
of you hiring a few great voice actors
and mocap artists than they do from you
hiring a suite of engineers working on
new shaders or lighting techniques. But
it's actually more than that. Somewhere
along the way, we and AAA got so
obsessed with all of the ways to make
our games 1% better. We forgot to add
that first 80% that made them good in
the first place. AAA is all about
polish, but polish isn't enough. Double
A has to focus on core gameplay because
if they don't deliver on that, they're
dead. And publishers are starting to
realize it. They still want giant
returns, but some are starting to see
that they're better off making 10
smaller bets on double A studios than
gambling it all on one gigantic AAA
title. And if this continues, double A
will become the new AAA. And I'd like to
be the first to welcome our new double A
overlords.
