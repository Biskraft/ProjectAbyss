Back in 2000, you could sew with your
Game Boy. Yeah, no joke. There was a
sewing machine peripheral. Though, it is
a little arguable whether the Game Boy
or the sewing machine is the peripheral
because the sewing machine was
functional without the Game Boy. But
regardless, boy oh boy, could you stitch
up a storm with a Game Boy Color
attached named the New Y, yes, I will be
yelling every time I say it. It was
actually kind of genius because at the
time portable computers were expensive
and custom computing hardware was even
more expensive. But you know what
wasn't? Gameboys, baby. And you don't
actually need a lot of horsepower to run
stitching patterns. So, someone had the
genius idea of making the new sewing
machine with a Game Boy Link cable port.
From there, the software could easily be
put on standard Game Boy Color carts and
controlled with a D-pad. Then, all of a
sudden, you had the power of
professional sewing right at home.
Sadly, for the New Year, computing got
better, programmable sewing machines got
cheaper, and Nintendo stopped making
Game Boy Color Pockets. So, by the
mid200s, the proud and mighty New Year
was no more. And stitchers forgot that.
