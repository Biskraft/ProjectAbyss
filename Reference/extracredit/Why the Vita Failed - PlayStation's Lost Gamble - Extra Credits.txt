Before we start, big thank you to John for popping in and do guest art again
I think we can all agree that from a commercial stand point the playstation Vita is a failure
Right?
I mean don't get me wrong, I love the Vita and I will play Persona on a handheld all day long
But at this point there is no way we can look at the Vita as anything but a commercial flop
We can only keep saying just give it a few months and it will turn around eventually for so long
The Vita is a failure
Even all that PS4 interactivity can't save it
The harsh reality is that the Vita has sold 4 million units
And it's closest competitor, the 3DS
Has sold 43 million units
That's not even a race anymore
It hasn't even managed 10% of Nintendo's 3DS sales
So now is a reasonable time to ask
"Why did the Vita fail so badly?"
I mean the PSP didn't come close to the original DS' astronomical numbers either
But at least it still achieved a respectable 80 million units sold by the end
Ofcourse there is no one answer to this
You could argue that the Vita's high launch prize hurt it
Though that's equally true of the 3DS
You could also argue that the rise of mobile gaming took a big bite out of it's market
Though again
Equally true of the 3DS
You can argue that developers didn't know what to do with many of it's unique features
But cmon that was true of the original DS too and that thing sold like crazy
But in the end I think the answer is much simpler than all that and a lot more counter-intuitive
If I had to give one reason why the Vita failed
I would say it was because the Vita was a way better machine than it ever should've been
I know that sounds strange, but stick with me here
Normally portable games are sold as roughly 2/3 the price of their console counter parts
Console games tended to go for $60
And heldhand games often go for $35 to $40
And due to the lack of ability to port between multiple platforms when creating for handhelds
Portable systems tend to have a smaller market than console games
But portable games tend to be much much cheaper to make
So it all evens out
And here is where the Vita failed
Look at the production values in any 3DS game
On that system the graphic capabilities are somewhere above the N64
But below the Game Cube
This is the realm of, what I'd call, double A developers
Working on budged below $10 million
And with teams fewer than 20 people
Now compare that to the Vita
Sony's enitre sales pitch for the Vita was that it was gonna to offer us better graphics than the PS2
Perhaps even comparable to the PS3
And holy cow they delivered
There is no arguement
These are some good looking games for a handheld
But those visuals come with a cost
Vita games are substantially more expensive to develop than 3DS games
And that started the spiral that killed the system
You see developers didn't want to take a risk on the fledgling system
Because of the difficulty and the expensive devoloping for it
It was just a too big of a gamble for people working in the hendheld market
Wagering on a Vita game was just not a bet that many of these companies could make
Without wagering the future of their whole studio
If they even had that the resources to make that wager in the first place
This lead to a drought of games for the platform
It only takes a quick glance to see that there are a lot more games being released on the 3DS
Than the Vita has been getting
And without games a system is nothing
We've said it before but I might as well repeat it here
Software not specs make a system
Thus began the death spiral
There was no software, so no one bought the system
Because no one bought the system it became even harder to convice small developers
To take the comparatively large risk of developing a Vita game
Which meant even fewer studios making software for the Vita
Which meant even fewer people had a reason to buy the system
Which in turn with each passing month
Made the gamble of developing for the Vita look less and less reasonable
So in the end
The PS Vita fell prey to Sony's over ambition
And when you put it side-by-side with the 3DS or even the IPhone
It serves as an almost comically good parable for the fact that players want games
Not graphics
Was there anything Sony could have done to save the Vita?
Perhaps
Could they still save it at this point?
Probably not
I don't think that Sony will and I don't think it would even be wise for them to try at this point
But they could've done what many console companies do
And in fact what they do with the playstation home consoles
And heavily underwrite development for the system
After all this is essentially what nintendo did with the DS
They just did it in-house
They payed for the development of enough quality software for the system to create confidence in the consumer
And to boost adoption of the device
With increased adoption came an increased willingness from other developers to built games for the platform
Ofcourse the DS was way cheaper to develop games for
So that reluctance was much easier to overcome
But theoretically this could've worked for Sony too
Unfortunately I am 100% certain that the folks at Sony are well aware of that option
And I think they tried it to some degree
But given development cost for the system they probably have decided
That shelling out enough money to underwrite enough games to give the system a chance
Would just be digging themselfs into a deeper financial hole than just writing it off and moving on
I think everyone at Sony, and in fact most of us as fans of games in general
Hope that some of the things they're doing with PS4 integration and the playstation shop
Will somehow turn things around for the system
And make it a platform everyone wants to develop for
But at the end of the day it's much more likely that the Vita is headed the way of the Neo-Geo
Ironically defeated by it's own quality
But here's to hoping
And best wishes to all those who are trying to make the most out of this excelent system
