In honor of Night Reigns DLC dropping
today, here is my quick treatise on what
the precise secret sauce is that made
this game sore while other multiplayer
sequels sank. Precedent. Even though us
souls dgens have the stigma of being a
bunch of lonewolf get good gatekeepers,
the truth is From Soft games from Demon
Souls to Elden Ring have always
encouraged jolly cooperation for
cooperation's sake with oftentimes no
tangible reward other than the
satisfaction of helping another player
on a boss. Heck, I know I've camped with
my summoning sign outside of the godskin
duo a bunch because screw those two
chuckleheads in particular. And sure, in
Night Ring, there are still occasional
bad actors and people that BM by
slamming that ping button over and over
and over again. But more often than not,
whether I'm playing with friends or a
rando Wilder named Dumbster, they're
going to throw themselves into the jaws
of the equilibrious beast's madness to
revive me when I'm down. Side note, if
you're watching this, thanks buddy. And
perhaps that's the reason Night Rain
succeeds. It's live together, died
together mentality. other souls have
trained us to be open to to even the
odds for more than a decade. So I guess
what I am saying is FromSoft has
actually been training us to get good at
friendship this whole time. And for a
deeper dive into all of this, follow
