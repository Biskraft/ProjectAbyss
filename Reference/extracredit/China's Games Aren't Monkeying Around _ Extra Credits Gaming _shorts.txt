The US games industry is not ready for the 
wrecking ball that is China. We've always  
considered it a big market, of course, but 
like every other industry that enters China,  
we're not even remotely prepared for them 
to make the switch from a place we use for  
outsourcing to being major producers in their own 
right, and that's already happened. For instance,  
did you know that more people now use Steam in 
Chinese than in English? Yep! Not to mention  
titles like Black Myth: Wukong did sales numbers 
in China alone that rivaled many of the biggest  
worldwide releases. A company called Epyllion 
just did an excellent report on the state of the  
industry, and in it they found that 42% of all 
spending growth in the industry in the past 14  
years was on games produced in China, and that 
15% of all growth came from people outside of  
China spending on Chinese games. So we really 
shouldn't keep ignoring China as the Western  
game development industry goes down in flames. 
Look China's investment in game publishers and  
dev studios has been going on for a while, but if 
western studios don't start paying more attention  
to their games and get themselves prepared, that 
part of the industry risks being left behind.
