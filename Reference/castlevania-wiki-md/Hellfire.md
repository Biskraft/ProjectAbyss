# Hellfire

is a spell in the *Castlevania* series. It is a powerful attack that summons three (or more) fireballs that are usually launched in a straightforward fashion, with the user generally swishing their cape/cloak open to launch the fireballs. It is well known for being Dracula's main and most commonly used attack, appearing nearly in every game.

## Overview
In some incarnations (most notably in *Symphony of the Night*), Hellfire is considered to be both the actual fireball attack and the teleportation that precedes it. In later games, Hellfire is instead two waves of three fireballs, and one wave of five or even more fireballs (like in *Order of Ecclesia*). In Soma's case, he fires a single wave of three fireballs, although there is a 1/3 chance than the spell will shoot out five fireballs instead.

Although the attack is mainly used by Dracula, it has also been used by Walter Bernhard, Alucard, Graham Jones and Soma Cruz. It is used as a spell by both, Desmond Belmont and Rohan Krause. Furthermore, some incarnations of Hellfire (most notably in *Symphony of the Night* and *Harmony of Despair*) overlap with Dark Inferno, considering the latter to be merely a more powerful variation of the former (although in *Harmony of Despair*, this is only true for Alucard; Charlotte's spell list considers Hellfire and Dark Inferno to be completely separate Hunter Skills). In the hands of Alucard, this spell becomes even more powerful, since he can use it to get away from an enemy and then perform one of his other spells. In *Dawn of Sorrow*'s Julius Mode, Alucard loses his ability to teleport (and the invincibility it provides) while casting the spell; the fireballs no longer travel in a straight line, but each one spaces out from the others as they move further. Alucard can cancel the spell at any moment either by backdashing or by performing a high jump.

Although the incarnation of Dracula from *Castlevania: Circle of the Moon* does not actually use Hellfire, his summoning bats to attack the player does have some similarities to Hellfire in attack pattern.
