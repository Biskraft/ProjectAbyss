# Sapphire

right|73px
The **Sapphire** is a blue jewel in *Castlevania*. It often appears in a ring (the **Sapphire Ring**) and is associated with the Ice element and Constitution.

## Game specific information
### *Vampire Killer*
The **Saphire Ring**<sup><nowiki>[</nowiki>sic<nowiki>]</nowiki></sup> allows for temporary invincibility, similar to the Blue Crystal (or the Invisibility Potion in other games), with the added effect that it also destroys any enemies that Simon touches while wearing it. It does not cause immunity to spiked traps, though.

### *Castlevania: Harmony of Dissonance*
Sapphires can only be sold to the Merchant in this game. They're worth $500, which is more than a Turquoise ($200), but less than a Ruby ($600).

### *Castlevania: Lament of Innocence*
Using a Sapphire with the Jewel Crush will damage any nearby enemies and transfer HP to Leon from each hit dealt out.

### *Castlevania: Order of Ecclesia*
The Sapphire is an item that by itself does nothing. It is needed for Laura's "A Pleasant Accessory" quest. If you bring her a Sapphire, an Emerald and a Ruby, she'll make the Sapphire Ring (greater Ice offensive power) and Sapphire Pins (greater defense against Ice) available at Jacob's shop, as well as the rings and pins of the other jewels.

## Other Sapphire items
The Opal Circlet is called the **Sapphire Circlet** in the original Japanese versions of *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*. It allows Alucard to absorb Ice-based damage.

The Sapphire Corset is the only corset in *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin* that also gives a CON boost in addition to a DEF boost.

In *Castlevania Judgment*, the Star-Sapphire Bracelet can be worn to customize the appearance of a character, although it has no effect gameplay-wise.
