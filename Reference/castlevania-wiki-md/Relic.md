# Relic

are special and rare items that provide new abilities or activate particular effects when acquired. They are usually found after defeating the boss of an area. A relic usually can be turned ON or OFF via the game's in-game menu, without consuming any resources. This can be helpful to suit a player's particular way of progressing through the game (e.g.: turning the Faerie Scroll off to remove the enemies' name window and this way have more area of vision on the screen, or capping the hero from a special ability to make the game harder).

In some games, functions characteristic to certain relics are obtained by different means. For example, in the *Sorrow* games, Soma Cruz can obtain the same abilities by gaining dominance over certain enemies' souls.

### *Castlevania: Circle of the Moon*
In *Circle of the Moon*, relics were referred to as **Magic Items**. Other than the name change, they are found and function exactly as the relics from other games, and even feature demonstrative clip-arts when picked. Although in this game, there's no way to turn them off.

### *Castlevania: Lament of Innocence*
Out of all the relics in *Lament of Innocence*, the Svarog is the only item that forces the player to use, primarily for tutorial purposes. All relics in this game are cast, toggled and consume MP. None of these are actually mandatory for completing the game. In fact, the player can finish the story with only the Svarog in possession (but missing out completion rates, and secret areas, including the Prison of Eternal Torture).
