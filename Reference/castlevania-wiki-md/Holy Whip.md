# Holy Whip

right|50px

The **Holy Whip** is a whip imbued with holy light.

## Game specific information
### *Castlevania: Circle of the Moon*
It can be created in *Circle of the Moon* through the Hunter Whip's magical attunement properties by combining the Mercury card with the Unicorn card. It restores HP with each attack.

### *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*
Found in the Infinite Corridor exclusively in Trevor Mode (thus it can only be used by Trevor Belmont). The Holy Whip is the only weapon in that gameplay mode that can kill Blood Skeletons (acting as a counterpart to the Purify spell from Hector's quest).

### *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin*
In *Portrait of Ruin*, the **Undead Killer** is nearly on par with the Vampire Killer in terms of strength and holy power. The Undead Killer's greatest asset is that it can permanently kill Red Skeletons and Red Axe Armors. It can be acquired by completing "S Rank Hunter" quest.

The Undead Killer's Critical Art in *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin* is very similar to the Vampire Killer in that the damage dealt is doubled and adds the Fire attribute. This makes the Undead Killer effective against highly resistant enemies, such as a Final Guard.

Strangely, if one is using this whip on Vincent when he has been afflicted with vampirism, it will cure him of the aliment, just as if Sanctuary had been cast. Why it would be able to do so is unknown, as its power lies in destroying what would otherwise be impossible to kill.

### *Akumajō Dracula: Kabuchi no Tsuisoukyoku*
In the novel, Curtis Lang, an apprentice of Julius Belmont, uses a whip by this name, which is less powerful than the Vampire Killer.

### *Castlevania: Lords of Shadow*
In *Lords of Shadow*, a Holy Whip that regenerates Gabriel's health with each landed strike can be created by activating Light Magic.
