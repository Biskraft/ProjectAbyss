# Agunea

right|245px

A strange but powerful weapon, the **Agunea** (or **Agnea**) fires a small, nearly-invisible projectile straight ahead. Once it hits, the attack button can be held down and multiple bolts of lightning will strike the target.


## Usage
### *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*
It is an effective weapon against many bosses in *Symphony of the Night*, particularly the Succubus. One must be careful while using it, however, as it consumes 5 Hearts per bolt summoned, and bolts emit in rapid succession, which may deplete the player's heart reserves in just a few seconds. Richter's item crash produces a large burst of electricity.

There are four Agunea to be found in the entire game, spread across both castles. It has been described as a "lightning ring", a "taser", or even as a derringer type pistol by different people, although there's no definitive answer as to just what the Agunea is.

### *Castlevania: Lament of Innocence*
Agunea can be used by combining the Cross sub-weapon with the White Orb.

### *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*

  - Agnea** is a skill which the Mage-Type Innocent Devil, Ogre Rod uses. It consists of blasting several enemies in front of the Innocent Devil with continuous Thunder damage. This ability consumes 20 Hearts with each cast.

### *Castlevania Puzzle: Encore of the Night*
The Agunea is a spell that can be used by Alucard.

## Trivia
- In Slavic mythology, Agunea is the youngest son of Svarog, a fire god often equated with Hephaestus. Agunea's role is to purify and protect the righteous with flames from "heaven", which in *Castlevania* is interpreted as lightning.
- Alucard's lightning attack while jumping in *Castlevania Judgment* is most likely inspired by his Agunea sub-weapon in *Symphony of the Night*, although it cannot be continuously applied.
