# Magic Missile

right|290px
The **Magic Missile** is a bow and arrow that is summoned behind the player. It hovers in the air, the string is pulled back and then an arrow is fired straight ahead. It is usually fired straight forward, but some variants can be launched diagonally or may home-in on an enemy.

## Game specific information
### *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*
Magic Missiles are abundant in this game; where they first appeared as a usable item (hence, a one-use weaponry). These are found in secret areas of the castle, dropped by the Sniper of Goth, or can be bought from the Master Librarian for $300.

The special attack from the Sword of Dawn can summon an archer.

### *Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow*
Soma Cruz can summon a bow in *Aria of Sorrow*, which produces a similar attack to the Magic Missile. This attack has similar animation to the one from *Symphony of the Night*.

### *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow* / *Castlevania: Harmony of Despair*
Soma is able to obtain a soul called **Summon Archer** by stealing the Amalaric Sniper's soul, and it functions similarly to its version in *Aria of Sorrow*, the only difference being the presence of the Amalaric Sniper.

An alternative soul, **Buster Shot**, produces a similar effect. However, it is Soma who brandishes the bow and, while holding the R button, the shot can gain power to cause more damage. This soul can be obtained by stealing the Skull Archer.

### *Castlevania: Order of Ecclesia*
Shanoa is able to use Magic Missiles known as **Arcus** (Latin for *bow*) Glyphs. But for her, it appears as if she is the one pulling the strings. If she moves away from her position before the arrow is fired, the bow continues to pull on its own though. Shanoa's main attacks on the ground in *Castlevania Judgment* is a bow attack. When used in the air, she will fire a spread of three arrows.
