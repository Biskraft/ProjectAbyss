# Curry

thumb|150px|<p style="text-align: center;">*Harmony of Despair* gamerpic.</p>
  - Curry** (sometimes referred to as **Curry Rice** or **Beef Curry**) is a useful food item in several *Castlevania* games. It's sometimes used as an ability called Delicious Curry where the character throws plates of curry at foes.

## Game specific information
### *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*
  - Curry Rice** recovers 28 HP in *Symphony of the Night*, which is one more than Spaghetti but one less than a Gyros Plate.

### *Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow*
It's used as weapon by the Waiter Skeleton. Soma can also gain this ability, called Delicious Curry, by gaining control of the Waiter Skeleton's soul. When used as a weapon, plates of curry can be thrown to damage enemies; however, they cannot be used as a normal healing item.

  - Beef Curry** is occasionally dropped by Waiter Skeletons after they have been defeated by the player. It heals 800 HP (more than any other food item).

### *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*
The **Beef Curry** item, Waiter Skeleton, and the Delicious Curry ability return in the sequel to *Aria of Sorrow*; however, this time Delicious Curry can leveled up. When it reaches Level 3, Soma will throw a much larger plate of curry. When thrown, a different sprite will also replace the Beef Curry sprite.

The Yeti seems to have a fondness for Beef Curry, fondness which Soma can use against it. In the very first room of the Lost Village, a mysterious humanoid creature can be seen lurking through the woods in the background. By dropping a plate of curry to lure it out (using the Delicious Curry ability), the creature will jump into the foreground, allowing Soma to defeat it and probably steal its soul.

### *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*
You can steal a **Curry** in the second battle against Trevor.

### *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin*

It's simply referred to as **Curry** in this game and it's a rare food item, dropped only by Fake Trevor in the Nest of Evil. In this game it's referred to as an Indian style dish.

### *Castlevania: Order of Ecclesia*
Once again it's referred to simply as **Curry**. For the first time it can be bought at a shop and is never dropped by an enemy. It appears in the shop as a reward for supplying Aeon with Tasty Meat so he can use it in his cooking. As in *Portrait of Ruin*, chef Aeon's curry is cooked and prepared Indian style.
