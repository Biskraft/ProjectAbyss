# Shogun Helmet

The **Shogun Helmet** is Nobunaga's helmet in *Castlevania*.

In *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin*, it gives 10 Defense, 3 Strength, but comes with a -5 Intelligence penalty. This is not as much as Defense as the Skull Mask (+13 DEF), which does not have a Strength boost, but has smaller penalties across for each of the non-physical statistics (-3 Intelligence, Luck, and Mind).

The Shogun Helmet returns in *Harmony of Despair*. For the most part, the Shogun Helmet offers roughly the same stats, but in addition to the Intelligence penalty (though this does help with sub-weapon grinding in Survival Mode as a whip user, since their sub-weapons level up by hits not damage), it also inflicts one of the same value on the character's Constitution.

It is similar to the other Japanese helmet that appears in *Castlevania*, the Masakado's Helm, which sacrifices luck instead of intelligence in *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*.

## Etymology
Shogun was the title applied to the military dictator from about the 8th century to the end of the 12th century. Later it was applied to the hereditary de-facto rulers of Japan, a position held by several families until 1868. Contrary to the item's description, the real Nobunaga never held the title of shogun. In reality, he was a daimyo (feudal lord) and warlord from the Warring States period (c. 1467 – c. 1603). His power and influence was greater than the actual shogun at the time, Ashikaga Yoshihide, who was restricted in power by Nobunaga and used as a puppet.
