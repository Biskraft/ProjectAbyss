The **Flying Humanoid** is an enemy in *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*. It is a mysterious figure that hovers in the distance.

## Origins
The Flying Humanoid is derived from an actual unexplained phenomenon that occurs even today with sightings of what appear to describe flying men or women, either strapped with some kind of strange device or equipment or alighted on a pair of unusual wings (either made from a fur-like substance or other material). Mexican observers and media often call it "la Bruja", or "the Witch". In fact, a careful look at the Flying Humanoid while it is in the background does seem to show a person, or at least a cape or dress flowing in the air.

Sightings of flying humanoids are not as common as other sky phenomena, but they do happen periodically. Explanations for this include: stray weather surveillance equipment/balloons, airplanes, flying animals, or even demonic creatures.

## Description
The Flying Humanoid resembles a cloaked figure that can be seen flying in the background right outside of Zephyr's boss room in the Cursed Clock Tower. As the creature's picture in the Enemy List's entry is shown at the same distance as it appears in the room where it is found at, its true appearance is hard to determine.

It can only be harmed by using the Mandragora's soul, Last Scream, which causes it to shriek and apparently explode, releasing its soul toward the player. As it has no rarity level in the Enemy List (and being the only monster to have no rarity level in the game), its soul can be acquired with every kill.

The soul received from the Flying Humanoid is a Guardian Soul, and it grants the Blessed Wings ability. When cast, the player is given a bonus to INT and LCK.

The Flying Humanoid is one of three cryptid-based hidden monsters in *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*, along with the Yeti and Mothman.
