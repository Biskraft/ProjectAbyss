# Alucart Mail

right|110px

The **Alucart Mail** is a body armor that resembles family armor but offers terrible defense. It was introduced in the 1997 game *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*.

## Game specific information
### *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*

The Alucart Mail provides a very minor DEF +2 bonus, but when it is equipped with the Alucart Sword and the Alucart Shield, Alucard gains LCK +30 and enters "Alucart" status. Its sprite is identical to the Alucard Mail's.

### *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*
{{Q|Look carefully, one letter is different! It's not who you think! Also offers terrible defense. As it is made of gold, it may be worth something though.|Description in *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*}}
The Alucart Mail returns in the 2005 game *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*, where it can be created through Weapon Combining by adding Midas' Gold to a Half Plate, which significantly reduces its DEF points from +24 to +1. Creating it should be avoided, as it cannot be upgraded further.

## Trivia
- The Alucart Mail does not appear in *Castlevania: Harmony of Despair*, even though the Alucart Shield and Alucart Sword do make it in the game. This makes it impossible to gain the "Alucart" status LCK bonus in that game.
- One possible explanation of its existence is when Hector creates it in *Castlevania: Curse of Darkness*, yet this doesn't explain how the Alucart Shield and the Alucart Sword were created.﻿
