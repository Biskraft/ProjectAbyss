# Weretiger

The **Weretiger** is an enemy in the *Castlevania* series. It is a werebeast resembling an anthropomorphic tiger.

## Origins
The weretiger is a type of lycanthrope, most often female, that is similar to a normal tiger in its habitat. Its first thumb|left|250px|<p style="text-align: center;">Weretiger as depicted in *[[wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons|Dungeons & Dragons* media.</p>]]appearance was in the original *Dungeons & Dragons* "white box" set from 1974, and later in the *Monster Manual* from 1977, eventually becoming a player character race in 1979.

In 1992, it finally became a player character class, with abilities like being able to speak with great cats, and magically roar to paralyze its enemies with fear.

## Description
The Weretiger first appeared in *Castlevania* (N64) and *Castlevania: Legacy of Darkness*, being the largest and most powerful of all werebeast enemies in both games, albeit also slow and plodding.
The Were-Tiger is an anthropomorphic tiger warrior. Its large body is covered in orange and black fur, and it has a small white mane across its face.

A smaller version later reappeared in *Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow*, where it's simply a palette-swap of the Werejaguar, with which, according to its enemy list entry description, holds some sort of rivalry.

## Trivia
- The Weretiger's enemy list entry description in *Aria of Sorrow* appears to make reference to Sagat, a character from the *Street Fighter* series, seeing as both share an uppercut move and a rivalry with a fellow pugilist; not to mention the common tiger motif.
  - His rivalry with the Werejaguar is a reference to Adon, another fighter from the series who uses a similar moveset and has a jaguar motif.
