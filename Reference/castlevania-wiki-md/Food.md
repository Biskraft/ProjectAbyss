# Food

- Food** can be consumed to recover health in the *Castlevania* series. In classic-style games, health is recovered immediately when the food item is obtained. In newer-style games, these edibles can be stored in an inventory and used at a moment of one's choosing.

## Description
In earlier games, food was usually some kind of meat. *Castlevania: Rondo of Blood* introduced other types of food which Maria Renard could eat instead. *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night* was the first game to have a wide variety of food types which could be collected in an inventory and used at a later time.

Some food is hazardous to ones health and will either reduce HP or even poison whomever eats it. In later games, some food will also increase the amount of MP one has.

Also in older games such as *Symphony of the Night*, food must first be equipped and then tossed out by using the respective button. The user must then rush over to the food item to effectively eat it and heal up before it flickers and disappears; multiple food items can be consumed like this via tossing out others repeatedly. Later games however, instead have food along with potions and other non-equip-able items be consumed automatically through their own menu upon selection.

In *Castlevania: Harmony of Despair*, many food items have a side benefit of increasing other attributes in addition to replenishing HP or MP.
