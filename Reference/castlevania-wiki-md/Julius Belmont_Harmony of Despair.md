  - Julius Belmont** appears as a playable character in *Castlevania: Harmony of Despair*. He is purchased as DLC in the Xbox 360 version, but comes free with the purchase of the full game for PlayStation 3.

## Overview
Perhaps the strongest vampire hunter to ever live, it was Julius Belmont who killed Dracula once and for all during the Demon Castle War in 1999 with the aid of Alucard, the Belnades clan, and the Hakuba family by sealing Dracula's Castle into a solar eclipse ending his cycle of resurrection. Julius appears in *Harmony of Despair* much as he did in *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*.

## Attack
Julius's Vampire Killer is enhanced from previous games and from what Jonathan Morris is able to do with it. It can be swung in eight directions, much as Simon Belmont could do in *Super Castlevania IV*. It can also latch onto the same magnetic hooks that Shanoa is able to use her Magnes ability on, and can swing off of them. It is also longer and somewhat quicker than it is for Jonathan.


## Sub-weapons
Julius is able to utilize any "Belmont subweapons" that have been used by any member of the Belmont family in a previous game. ## Martial Arts
Julius can gain the martial art Uppercut, though he needs to be closer to an enemy in order to use it than Jonathan does. He also gain a unique martial art called Omnia Vanitas. This is the teleporting move he used in Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow, which was missing from Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow.
