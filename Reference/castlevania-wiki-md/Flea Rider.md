# Flea Rider

The **Flea Rider** is an enemy in the *Castlevania* series. They are Flea Men riding on specially trained war-geese.

## Description
Flea Riders are Flea Men who ride on specially trained war-geese. When controlled by a Flea Man, the goose flies in erratic patterns rather than the straightforward path it would otherwise take. The goose and Flea Man tend to be destroyed together.

## Appearances
### *Castlevania: Rondo of Blood*
Flea Riders are encountered all along the deck of the Ghost Ship. They soar in from the sky in endless numbers (although there are usually no more than two on the screen at a time) and fly around the hero in a somewhat erratic manner, albeit still on their trail. They can become a real nuisance while dealing with the many Heavy Armors that are also found in that area.


### *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*
Flea Riders are encountered in the Castle Keep. They swoop in and start hunting the player. They are initially found stationary on their spawning spots in midair and will begin their chase when approached. Since some of them are found in precarious areas that are difficult to traverse by normal means, it's advisable to lure them down to ground level first, where fighting them is easier.

## Trivia
- The goose is a slightly modified sprite of the Eagle seen in *Castlevania Chronicles*.
- This enemy is originally called **Semler** in Japan. This is probably a reference to Johann Salomo Semler, a historian who lived in the 18 century. After having a dispute with one of his students, Gotthold Ephraim Lessing, the latter wrote a letter where he called Semler an "*impertinente Professorengans*", which roughly translates to "impertinent Professor goose".
