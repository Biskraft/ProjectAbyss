# Hidden Item

right

  - Hidden Items** are special items appearing in many *Castlevania* titles that, as their name state, are hidden somewhere within the levels of the game, either behind a breakable wall or by performing a specific action. They are usually worth many points, should the player manage to find them; or can also be acquired just for inventory completion purposes, as they may not have any other use in that game. In most games, these items can be treasures or mascots from other Konami franchises.

## Overview
Hidden items first appeared in the original *Castlevania*. They rise up from the ground as the player walks over a certain area or stays/kneels for a certain period of time in a specific spot. They then have to hurry and collect the item before it disappears. Hidden items would return in that game's remake, *Castlevania Chronicles*.

Normal items, such as food, weapons, money bags, etc., could be obtained in unusual ways in *Castlevania: Rondo of Blood*, such as by ringing a bell. Methods like these would become common ways to obtain hidden items in later games. *Castlevania: Harmony of Dissonance* hid a large collection of furniture in the game that could be used to decorate a special room. Hidden Items involving treasures or mascots became a regular feature of modern *Castlevania* games starting in *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*, and would also appear in *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin*, *Order of Ecclesia*, and *Harmony of Despair*.

### *Castlevania: Dawn of Sorrow*
After a long absence, traditional Hidden Items (treasures) reappeared in *Dawn of Sorrow*. Konami Man made his debut as a hidden item in this game as well - the first mascot item since the Moai statue which could also be considered a treasure.

These items can be sold at the shop for 2,500G. Since the selling price in this game is half the real value of the items, their real value would be 5,000G, in accordance to what their description state.


### *Castlevania: Portrait of Ruin*
There are no actual "hidden items" in this games, but the same items are used like trophies for defeating the game in hard mode. A different trophy is given depending on the level cap used. These are accompanied with permanent stat boosts.


### *Castlevania: Order of Ecclesia*
Hidden items can be found in breakable walls in this game.


### *Castlevania: Harmony of Despair*
Each chapter in *Harmony of Despair* has a single hidden item in it.


### *Castlevania: Lords of Shadow*
Fingers of Frankenstein can be collected in the Reverie expansion to *Lords of Shadow*.
